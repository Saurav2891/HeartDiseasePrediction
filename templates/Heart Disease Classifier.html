Heart Disease Test

Heart Disease Test Details

  • Typical Angina: Chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart.
  • Atypical Angina: Chest pain that differs from typical angina symptoms.
  • Non-anginal Pain: Chest pain not related to heart disease.
  • Asymptomatic: No chest pain or discomfort.

  • Normal: No abnormalities in the ECG results.
  • ST-T Wave Abnormality: Abnormalities observed in the ST-T wave segment of the ECG.
  • Probable or Definite Left Ventricular Hypertrophy: Indications of an enlarged left ventricle in the heart.
Exercise-induced angina refers to chest pain or discomfort that is triggered by physical exertion or exercise.

The shape or inclination of the ST segment during peak exercise on an electrocardiogram (ECG).
